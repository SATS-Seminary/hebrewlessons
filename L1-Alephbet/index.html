Izbet Sartah Abecedary (c.12th century B.C)
The earliest known Hebrew inscriptions are two abecedaries- the Izbet Sartah Ostracon and the Tel Zayit Inscription. These inscriptions present the alphebet with the pe before the ayin, a reversal from what we will learn. This pecularity is sometimes used to date certain Psalms, some of which use this letter order in Hebrew acrostics.

There are six consonants that have two different, but related sounds when they appear with a dot, called a daghesh.  These consonants are known as the “begadkephat” letters. It may be helpful to think of the daghesh as a kind of obstruction that stops the flow of air from your mouth.  The consonants without the daghesh do not stop, but kind of ‘leak’.

There are four consonants which are classified as gutturals.  These letters are called gutturals because they are pronounced at the back of the throat.  Though the letter ר (resh) will sometimes behave like a guttural, it is not classified as one.
