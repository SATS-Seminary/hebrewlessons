You might think it will take some time before you can say anything meaningful in Hebrew.  After all, we are just about to begin learning nouns and you haven’t even studied verbs!  How much can one say without a verb? Quite a lot, actually!   Hebrew has a knack for cramming lots of meaning into short, pithy phrases.  Perhaps one of the best known examples of this is the verbless clause that forms the content of the Shema: Hear O Israel, יהוה אלהינו יהוה אחד Seemingly countless volumes have been written on this four word, verbless Hebrew clause.  It has profound depth and meaning about who God is- and all without a single verb!
